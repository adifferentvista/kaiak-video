## 1. Best HeyGen Video Formats for LinkedIn

### Length That Works
- **Sweet spot: 45–75 seconds.** LinkedIn auto-plays in feed; you have ~3 seconds to hook, and most viewers drop off after 60s. Aim for 60s as default.
- **Under 30s** for quick tips/hot takes (high completion rate, algorithm loves it).
- **90s maximum** for tutorials or story-driven content. Anything longer loses feed viewers.

### Style & Production
- **Talking head, direct to camera.** HeyGen's strength is the avatar speaking naturally. Don't add excessive B-roll or text overlays that distract — keep it simple and authoritative.
- **1:1 square format** for LinkedIn feed (takes up more screen real estate than 16:9). If also repurposing for YouTube Shorts/Reels, record in 9:16 as well.
- **Captions are mandatory.** 80%+ of LinkedIn video is watched on mute. Use HeyGen's built-in captions or add SRT in post. Bold/highlight key phrases.
- **Clean background.** Solid color, subtle gradient, or a professional office setting. Avoid busy backgrounds — they reduce perceived authority.
- **One topic per video.** Never try to cover two ideas. One insight, one takeaway.

### Hook Patterns That Stop the Scroll (first 3 seconds)
1. **Contrarian statement:** "Most people are using ChatGPT wrong for content."
2. **Specific number:** "This 3-step workflow saves me 10 hours a week."
3. **Direct question:** "What if you could turn one blog post into 15 pieces of content?"
4. **Bold claim + qualifier:** "I automated 80% of my content pipeline — here's the tool stack."
5. **Pattern interrupt:** "Stop making AI do everything. Here's what to automate and what NOT to."

### Structure Template (for every video)
```
[0-3s]   HOOK — pattern interrupt or bold statement
[3-15s]  CONTEXT — why this matters, who it's for
[15-50s] VALUE — the insight, framework, or walkthrough
[50-60s] CTA — what to do next (comment, follow, link in comments)
```

## 3. Positioning Framework: Authority Without Overclaiming

### The "Practitioner, Not Professor" Frame
Benedict's biggest asset is that he's learning in public while DOING real work. This is more relatable and trustworthy than claiming guru status. Use these framing patterns:

| Instead of... | Say... |
|---|---|
| "I'm an AI expert" | "I build AI workflows every day" |
| "I'll teach you AI" | "Here's what I tested this week" |
| "This is the best tool" | "This is what worked for my use case" |
| "You need to do this" | "Here's what changed when I tried this" |
| "Trust me, I know" | "I've built 30+ automations — here's the pattern" |

### The Authority Stack (things that signal expertise without claiming it)
1. **Specificity.** Say "I use n8n with Claude's API to auto-categorize support tickets" not "I use AI to automate things." Details signal experience.
2. **Process, not just results.** Show the messy middle — "This broke twice before I figured out the right trigger" builds trust.
3. **Numbers.** "Saves 10 hours/week" or "processed 500 documents" is more credible than "it's really helpful."
4. **Stacking credentials implicitly.** In your LinkedIn bio and video descriptions, reference "1,000+ educators trained" and "50+ organizations served" — let the numbers speak.
5. **Honest limitations.** "This doesn't work well for X" makes everything else you say more believable.

  - "I build AI workflows that save teams 10+ hours/week | Automation • Content Repurposing • Knowledge Systems"
  - "AI automation for small teams & consultants | n8n, Claude, HeyGen | Sharing what I build every week"

## 5. LinkedIn Posting Strategy

### When to Post
- **Best days:** Tuesday, Wednesday, Thursday (highest professional engagement)
- **Best times:** 7:00–8:30 AM in your target audience's timezone. For a global audience, 7 AM EST / 7 PM WIB hits both US morning and Asia evening.
- **Monday is fine for a strong piece** — people are catching up on feeds.
- **Avoid:** Friday afternoon, Saturday, Sunday (unless you have a casual/personal brand post)

### Post Format (for video posts)
```
[HOOK LINE — same as video hook, in text]

[2-3 sentences of context — what the video covers and why it matters]

[Optional: bullet points of what they'll learn]

🎥 Watch the 60-second breakdown above.

[CTA: question to drive comments, or "DM me [KEYWORD]" for lead gen]

---

[3-5 hashtags]
```

### Hashtag Strategy
Use 3–5 per post. Mix broad reach with niche:
- **Broad:** #AIAutomation #Productivity #FutureOfWork #ContentStrategy
- **Mid:** #n8n #WorkflowAutomation #ContentRepurposing #KnowledgeManagement
- **Niche/Branded:** #BuildWithAI #AIWorkflows #KAIAK

### CTA Patterns That Work
| Goal | CTA |
|---|---|
| Engagement | "What's your biggest automation win? Drop it below." |
| Leads | "DM me 'WORKFLOW' and I'll send you the template." |
| Followers | "Follow me — I share a new automation every week." |
| Traffic | "Full breakdown in the comments 👇" (then post link as first comment) |

### Engagement Rules
- **Reply to every comment within the first 2 hours.** LinkedIn's algorithm heavily rewards early engagement.
- **Post the link in the first comment, NOT in the post body.** LinkedIn suppresses posts with external links.
- **Like 10-15 posts from your target audience BEFORE you post.** Primes the algorithm to show your content to those people.

## 6. Repurposing One HeyGen Video into Multiple Content Pieces

### The Repurposing Cascade (from one 60s video)

```
1 HeyGen Video (60s, 1:1)
│
├── LinkedIn video post (original)
├── YouTube Short / Instagram Reel (re-export in 9:16)
├── Twitter/X video post (trim to 30s for the punchiest segment)
│
├── LinkedIn text post (transcribe → rewrite as text-only post)
├── LinkedIn carousel (extract 4-5 key points → design in Canva)
│
├── Blog paragraph (embed video + expand the script into 300-500 words)
├── Newsletter snippet (2-3 sentences + link to video)
│
├── Audiogram (extract audio → add waveform visual → podcast clip)
├── Quote graphic (pull the best one-liner → branded image)
│
└── Thread (break the script into 4-5 tweet-length posts)
```

### Workflow to Execute This
1. **Record in HeyGen** — export in both 1:1 and 9:16
2. **Download transcript** from HeyGen (or run audio through Whisper)
3. **Feed transcript into Claude** with prompt: "Turn this 60-second video transcript into: (a) a LinkedIn text post, (b) 5 carousel slide headlines, (c) a tweet thread of 4 posts, (d) a 2-sentence newsletter blurb"
4. **Use Canva bulk create** with the carousel headlines (you already have this pipeline)
5. **Schedule everything** — Monday video, Tuesday text post, Wednesday carousel, Thursday newsletter mention
